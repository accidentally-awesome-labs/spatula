## Permitted Uses

### 1. Unmodified Official Releases

You may use the "Spatula" name to accurately refer to an **unmodified** official release
downloaded from the official Spatula distribution channels (npm, GitHub Releases, GHCR).
Example: "This product runs Spatula v1.0.0."

### 2. Attribution in Derivative Works ("Based on Spatula")

You may state that your project or product is **"based on Spatula"** or **"derived from
Spatula"** to credit the upstream project, provided:

- The "based on Spatula" attribution appears in a clearly subordinate position (e.g., in
  documentation, About pages, or release notes), not as the primary product name.
- Your project name, domain, and top-level marketing do NOT use "Spatula" as the main
  identifier (see Prohibited Uses below).

### 3. Nominative Fair Use

You may use the "Spatula" name to refer accurately to the Spatula project in articles,
blog posts, academic papers, conference talks, or documentation — provided the use is
truthful and does not imply endorsement by Accidentally Awesome Labs.

## Prohibited Uses

The following uses are NOT permitted without a separate written trademark license from
Accidentally Awesome Labs:

1. **Fork naming** — A fork, derivative work, or competing product may NOT use "Spatula"
   as its primary project name, repository name, or package name (e.g., `spatula-pro`,
   `spatula-enterprise`, `spatula-fork`). Choose a distinct name for your project.

2. **Domain names** — You may NOT register or use any domain that includes "spatula" in
   a way that could suggest official affiliation (e.g., `spatula.io`, `getspatula.com`,
   `spatula-hosting.com`). The official domain is `spatula.dev`.

3. **Marketing and branding** — You may NOT use the Spatula name or logo as the primary
   identifier in advertising, product marketing, or SaaS offerings in a way that could
   create confusion about the source or sponsorship of the product.

4. **Logo use** — The Spatula logo (wordmark, icon, and any associated brand assets in the
   `brand/` directory) is covered separately by `brand/LICENSE-BRAND.md` and is NOT
   licensed under the MIT License. All rights to the logo are reserved.

5. **Implied endorsement** — You may NOT use the Spatula name or Accidentally Awesome Labs
   name to imply endorsement, partnership, or certification of your product or service
   without prior written consent.
